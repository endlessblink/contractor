## Design Context

### Users
Contractor is for non-technical Hebrew-speaking users, especially freelancers and small service providers, who need to create client-ready quotes, contracts, and work orders without learning document formatting, legal clause management, or AI tooling.

### Brand Personality
The interface should feel simple, safe, and approachable. It should reduce anxiety around business documents, make AI feel helpful rather than technical, and build trust that the generated documents will look professional.

### Aesthetic Direction
Hebrew-first RTL, Heebo typography, and the existing Ink & Frost palette are the current visual foundation. The app can keep its calm teal-on-ink identity, but should avoid feeling like a generic dark AI dashboard. The preferred direction is practical, guided, and reassuring rather than futuristic, developer-centric, or visually noisy.

### Design Principles
1. Make the next action obvious for non-technical users at every step.
2. Use visual hierarchy to reduce cognitive load, especially in dense forms and clause-management screens.
3. Keep AI features human and task-oriented; avoid making the interface feel like a developer console.
4. Preserve Hebrew RTL clarity in layout, labels, document previews, and empty states.
5. Use color purposefully for state, progress, and confidence, not decoration.
